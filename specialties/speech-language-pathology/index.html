Speech & Language Pathology

Speech & Language Pathology

The speech-language pathologists in the Therapy Services department evaluate and treat speech, language and cognitive deficitsas well as oral, feeding, and swallowing skills.

View Locations
View Providers

Patients who would benefit from speech therapy are those with a history of difficulties with:

  • Dysphagia (Feeding/Swallowing)
  • Cognitive Communication (Memory/Attention/Problem solving)
  • Speech Articulation Disorders (Apraxia/Dysarthria/Phonological)
  • Aphasia (Understand or Express Language)
  • Social Communication
  • Voice (Correct volume and voice quality)
  • Fluency (Stuttering and Cluttering)
  • Augmentative and Alternative Communication AAC (Expressive and/or Language Comprehension Disorders)
